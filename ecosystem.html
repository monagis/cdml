Marine Ecosystems

The water quality of the sea plays a crucial factor in human health. Much of our food source is marine-based, especially for island states that depend heavily on rich, productive fishing inshore areas and offshore banks. Populated coastal regions are also being developed for tourism, land reclamation, port industries, energy production and shoreline hard engineering measures including breakwaters and the other coastal rehabilitation. Much of this development contributes directly to long-term imbalance of water quality and will adversely affect marine ecosystems in the long run. It becomes crucial to understand the underlying hydrodynamics in order better predict water quality distribution spatially and temporally. This also has implications for propagation of material introduced into our waters and influenced by the current flow structure along the coast. The regions of focus are Kingston Harbour, Portland Bight, the Port Royal Cays and Montego Bay.

Numerical Model Descriptions

RMA-11 is a finite element water quality model for simulation of three dimensional estuaries, bays, lakes, rivers and coastal regions. It is also capable of simulating one and two-dimensional approximations of systems, either separately or in combined form. It is designed to accept input of velocity fields from the three dimensional stratified flow model, RMA-10, and these are used in the solution of the advection diffusion constituent transport equations. RMA-11 can represent kinds of components based on the main interactions between the nurtrients related constituents. This model simulation can allow us to get water quality information such as BOD, DO, Org-N, NH3, NO2, NO3, Org-P, PO4, Chla, Sediment, Zooplankton and Inorganic carbon spatially and temporally.



On-Going Work

The results of these numerical experiments show important ecosystem parameters in the coastal marine ecosystem cycle. These simulations allow us to evaluate the marine ecosystem in the study region. This is especially important for populated and industrialized coastal areas where an imbalance in the ecosystem cycle by river discharge containing high nutrient (nitrogen, phosphorus) loads encourage red and blue tides. Using the ecosystem model, RMA-11, we are currently modelling the effects of water quality on the ecosystems of these areas.
